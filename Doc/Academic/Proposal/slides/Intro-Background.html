Introduction

Background

01

Internal messaging systems are widely used in organizations, particularly Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs), as the primary tool for daily professional communication and collaboration.

02

Although many platforms use encryption and multi-factor authentication, research shows that centralized credential storage remains vulnerable to data breaches and lacks real-time detection for insider threats. This concerns data confidentiality, access integrity, and privacy.

03

This project focuses on developing "SentriZK," a secure internal chat application that integrates Zero-Knowledge Proof (ZKP) authentication and on-device AI anomaly detection. The system aims to help SMEs eliminate credential theft risks and detect suspicious behavior locally.
