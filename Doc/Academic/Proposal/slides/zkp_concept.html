Where it Fits

ZKP is an evolution of Knowledge-Based authentication. It relies on "what you know" (like a password) but changes how that knowledge is verified.

The Core Difference

Unlike passwords where you give the secret to the server, ZKP allows you to prove you know the secret without ever sending it.

Why It Matters

Total privacy. The server verifies your identity mathematically (using Groth16), but your actual data never leaves your device.
